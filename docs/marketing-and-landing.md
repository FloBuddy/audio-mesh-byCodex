# Marketing, Design, And Landing Page Plan

## Target Customer

Primary early adopters:

- Multi-device households.
- Early users who own Apple devices today but want a solution that eventually connects everything.
- People who listen to podcasts, YouTube, lectures, livestreams, or music while moving around at home.
- Users who already own AirPods, Apple TV, iPad, or multiple Macs.
- Technical users frustrated by Bluetooth range and audio routing limitations.

Not the first customer:

- Competitive gamers.
- Musicians needing live monitoring.
- Enterprise AV installers.
- Audiophiles expecting bit-perfect multi-room sync from version one.

## Positioning

Audio Mesh is not "AirPlay but better." That claim invites the wrong comparison.

Better positioning:

> A local audio bridge for all your devices.

Supporting copy:

> Play audio from your Mac, pick it up on your iPhone, iPad, Apple TV, or another Mac, and keep listening through the device closest to you.

## Messaging Pillars

- Works like a speaker on your Mac.
- Starts with the Apple devices you already have, with a protocol designed for Windows, Linux, Android, TVs, and more.
- Built for room-to-room listening.
- LAN-first and private by default.
- Designed for podcasts, long videos, music, and passive listening.

## Naming

`Audio Mesh` is clear and technical. It may be good for the project and beta, but the consumer product name could eventually be warmer.

Name directions:

- Keep: Audio Mesh.
- Descriptive: Mesh Audio, LocalListen, Roam Audio.
- Apple-household angle: HomeCast, RoomLink Audio.

Avoid names that imply official Apple/AirPlay compatibility.

## Landing Page Structure

### Hero

Headline:

> Audio Mesh

Subheadline:

> Send your Mac's audio across your home and listen from the Apple device nearest you.

Primary CTA:

> Join the Mac + iPhone beta

Secondary CTA:

> See how it works

Hero visual:

- Realistic home scene with a MacBook streaming to iPhone and Apple TV.
- Avoid abstract wave graphics as the primary visual.
- Show actual devices and the listening path.

### Section 1: The Problem

Copy angle:

Bluetooth is tied to one device. AirPlay is built for speakers and rooms. Audio Mesh is for people moving through a home with the audio they started on their Mac.

### Section 2: How It Works

Three steps:

1. Choose `Audio Mesh` as your Mac output.
2. Open Audio Mesh on iPhone, iPad, Apple TV, or another Mac.
3. Listen through that device or its paired headphones.

### Section 3: Built For

- Podcasts while doing chores.
- Long YouTube videos away from the desk.
- Lectures and livestreams around the house.
- Apple TV or iPad as a local headphone bridge.

### Section 4: Honest Limits

State clearly:

- Best for passive listening.
- Not designed for gaming or calls yet.
- iPhone can receive audio, but cannot act as a system-wide source.
- Requires devices on the same local network.

### Section 5: Beta CTA

Collect:

- Email.
- Mac model and macOS version.
- Receiver devices owned.
- Router/Wi-Fi type if they know it.
- Primary listening use case.

## Visual Design Direction

The app should feel like a native utility, not a media brand.

Style:

- Clean macOS/iOS native controls.
- Compact status panels.
- Clear connection states.
- Minimal color, with one strong accent.
- Avoid decorative gradients and abstract audio-wave overload.

Important screens:

- Source menu bar popover.
- Receiver stream list.
- Now receiving screen.
- Pairing screen.
- Diagnostics screen.
- Empty state when no stream is found.

## Launch Strategy

### Pre-Beta

- Build in public with short demos.
- Recruit from Mac utility communities, audio forums, indie hacker circles, and multi-device households.
- Publish a technical explainer: "Why your iPhone can receive Mac audio but cannot be a system-wide source."

### Private Beta

- 25-50 users.
- Require feedback after first install.
- Prioritize varied routers and homes, not just friends with perfect Wi-Fi.
- Track install failures aggressively.

### Public Beta

- 250-500 users.
- Add referral from existing beta users.
- Publish compatibility matrix.
- Ship known-limits page.

### Launch

- Product Hunt / Hacker News only after installer and first-run UX are solid.
- App Store listing for receiver app.
- Direct macOS installer download.
- Consider Setapp or Paddle/Lemon Squeezy-style licensing later.

## Pricing Hypotheses

Start with beta free.

Potential pricing:

- One-time Mac license: $19-$39.
- Household license: $49-$79.
- Free receiver apps.
- Paid Pro later for multi-room sync, advanced diagnostics, and cross-platform support.

Avoid subscription until there is an ongoing service cost or premium feature set that justifies it.

## Support Strategy

Support pages needed before beta:

- Install and uninstall.
- Local Network permission.
- No streams found.
- Audio dropouts.
- Bluetooth headphones route troubleshooting.
- Latency explanation.
- Privacy and LAN behavior.
